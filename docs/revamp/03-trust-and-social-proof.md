# Trust & Social Proof Strategy

## Why This Is the #1 Business Priority

A visitor who lands on VyaptIX today cannot answer: "Has this worked for anyone like me?"

Without social proof, every visitor has to take the company's word for it. For a global B2B audience evaluating AI automation tools — where vendor failures are expensive — this creates a credibility gap that no amount of good design or clever copy can bridge.

## Social Proof Hierarchy (Most to Least Powerful)

1. **Named customer case study** (company name + person + before/after metric)
2. **Named testimonial with photo** (real person, real company)
3. **Named testimonial without photo** (real person, real company)
4. **Aggregate metrics** (X reviews collected, Y businesses served)
5. **Logo wall** (recognizable company logos)
6. **Industry/category list** ("used by restaurants, clinics, salons...")
7. **Anonymous testimonials** (real quotes, no name — weakest)

## What to Collect from the Team

Priority content requests (in order):

### Tier 1 (Do Before Launch)
- [ ] 3 named customer testimonials: full name, job title, company, explicit quote, photo permission
- [ ] 3 verified metrics: total reviews collected, total businesses, average customer time-to-review
- [ ] 1 mini case study: "[Business name] went from X to Y reviews in Z days"

### Tier 2 (Do Within 30 Days of Launch)
- [ ] 5–8 customer logo images (PNG with transparent background) + written permission to use
- [ ] Video testimonial from 1 customer (even a Loom screen recording works)
- [ ] 2 detailed case studies with quantified outcomes

### Tier 3 (Build Over Time)
- [ ] Press mentions or media coverage
- [ ] G2 / Capterra / Product Hunt reviews (build external credibility)
- [ ] Integration partner logos (WhatsApp Business, Google, etc.)

## Placement Strategy

| Page | Social Proof Element |
|---|---|
| Homepage hero | 4 metric stats (reviews, time, businesses, avg rating) |
| Homepage (below hero) | Customer logo wall |
| Homepage (mid-page) | 3 testimonial cards |
| AI Review Generator page | 1 mini case study + 2 testimonials specific to product |
| Contact page | 1 testimonial + 1 metric (reduces form anxiety) |

## What NOT to Do

- Do not use generic stock photos of "happy businesspeople" as testimonial avatars
- Do not use job titles from companies the target audience has never heard of
- Do not fabricate or exaggerate metrics
- Do not use testimonials without written permission
- Do not show testimonials where the name is First Name + Last Initial only — it reads as fake

## Interim Approach (If No Social Proof Is Ready at Launch)

If the team cannot gather named testimonials before launch, use this approach instead of fake content:

1. Remove testimonial section entirely (a gap is better than fake credibility)
2. Replace with an "Industries We Serve" grid with icons (already partially exists)
3. Add a single compelling metric if verifiable: "X Google reviews collected and counting"
4. Add "Trusted by local businesses across 12 industries" if verifiable
