> AI-powered tools that turn every customer interaction into a Google review, a qualified lead, or a closed deal — without adding headcount.

> ✦ Trusted by [X]+ businesses across [Y] industries

## About Page Narrative

The About page should tell a founder story, not corporate boilerplate.

**Narrative arc:**
1. What problem the founders personally experienced or witnessed
2. What they tried that didn't work
3. The moment that led to building VyaptIX
4. What VyaptIX has become
5. What's next

**Avoid:**
- Generic mission statements with no specificity
- "We believe in the power of innovation" type copy
- Stock photo team shots
- Buzzwords without substance

## Tone Examples

### Too corporate (avoid):
> "VyaptIX leverages cutting-edge artificial intelligence to deliver transformative customer engagement solutions that synergize operational efficiency with revenue acceleration."

### Too casual (avoid for B2B):
> "Yo, want more Google reviews? We got you lol"

### Right tone (confident + clear + warm):
> "Most businesses miss reviews not because customers don't want to leave them — but because leaving a review is harder than it should be. We built a tool that makes it take 20 seconds instead of 5 minutes. The result: businesses that used to get 2 reviews a month now get 20."

---

## Language Rules

1. Say "Google review" not "online review" — specificity builds trust
2. Say "under 20 seconds" not "fast" — numbers beat adjectives
3. Say "your customer" not "the customer" — personalization matters
4. Say "book a demo" not "get in touch" or "contact us" — specific asks get specific responses
5. Never say "world-class" or "cutting-edge" without evidence
6. Never say "seamlessly" — it's the most overused SaaS word
7. Never say "leverage" when "use" works fine
